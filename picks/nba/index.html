The Engine Behind the Picks

How Propeller Analyzes NBA Player Props

Eight specialized AI agents run in parallel on every NBA prop, each contributing a weighted signal. The result is a single confidence score that tells you how strong the edge is and which direction to take it — faster than any manual research process.

Injury Impact Agent

WEIGHT 4.0 — HIGHEST

The most influential signal in the entire system. The NBA Injury agent does not simply flag a player as OUT and move on — it models the full cascade of effects across the roster. When a star is ruled out, usage redistributes. The second option sees a significant volume surge. Role players who rarely started can suddenly log 34 minutes. Propeller traces exactly who absorbs those minutes and how each teammate's scoring, rebound, and assist volume shifts as a direct result. This agent re-checks injury reports multiple times per day so that late-breaking news before tip-off is captured before books finish adjusting. This is the primary reason AI NBA picks from Propeller differ meaningfully from simple trend tools.

Pace Analysis Agent

WEIGHT 2.0

Fast-paced teams generate more possessions per 48 minutes, directly inflating raw stat totals for every player on the court. The Pace agent combines home team pace rating, away team pace rating, and the Vegas game total to project the statistical environment before a single minute is played. A player on a run-and-gun team in a projected 230-point game is fundamentally positioned differently than the same player in a 200-point grind. This is especially relevant when evaluating best NBA player props today on points and assists.

Usage & Tracking Agent

WEIGHT 2.0

Usage rate alone tells an incomplete story. This agent layers in BallDontLie tracking data — touches per game, drives to the basket, paint touches, and time of possession — for top-minute players. A player with a high touch rate and consistent drive frequency has a different scoring and assists profile than a player with similar raw usage who operates primarily off movement. The NBA prop analyzer uses these signals to surface edges that box score averages miss entirely.

Minutes Trajectory Agent

WEIGHT 2.0

Minutes are the multiplier on every stat. The Minutes agent tracks each player's recent trajectory, detects role changes — a promotion to the starting lineup, a demotion to the bench, or an expanded role following an injury — and applies a blowout adjustment. Players on heavy favorites historically see shortened minutes in garbage time. Understanding whether a player will log 38 minutes or 26 minutes changes the entire prop evaluation on every stat line.

No-Vig Probability Agent

WEIGHT 2.0

Every sportsbook line includes a margin baked in. The NoVig agent strips that margin from both sides of a prop to calculate the true implied probability at every book. Cross-book disagreement — when one book prices a line significantly out of consensus — is flagged as a standalone edge indicator. Juice direction signals where sharp money has moved since the line opened. This is how NBA prop bets today are evaluated for market value, not just statistical merit.

Defensive Matchup

WEIGHT 1.5

Defense vs Position (DvP) analysis measures how the opponent has defended comparable players at the same position over the season. A wing defender locking down shot creators is a meaningful signal against a volume scorer's points prop, even when all other indicators are bullish.

Hit Rate & Trends

WEIGHT 1.5

Recency-weighted hit rate treats the last five games more heavily than the full-season sample. Hot and cold streaks are detected relative to each player's own baseline. Enhanced with hustle stats: deflection rates correlate to steals props, and contested shot frequency correlates to blocks props.

Rest & Fatigue

WEIGHT 0.8

Back-to-back games, three-in-four-night stretches, and four-in-five runs introduce measurable fatigue. High-usage veterans show consistent stat suppression on the second night of a back-to-back. The Rest agent flags schedule density and adjusts confidence on props for players with elevated fatigue exposure.

Ensemble Scoring

COMBINED

All eight agents contribute weighted scores that combine via weighted combination into a final model confidence score. Agents that return no signal for a given prop are skipped entirely — a neutral reading never dilutes a strong directional signal. Regression correction dampens overconfidence on noisy or small-sample statistics. See our full methodology.

The most effective NBA prop betting strategy centers on three pillars. First, injury and lineup awareness: props change dramatically when key players are out, and the cascading effect on teammates is often mispriced. Second, pace and game environment: high-total games between fast-paced teams create different volume floors than slow defensive contests. Third, market inefficiencies: books set lines based on public perception, not optimal probability, and cross-book disagreement reveals where value exists. Propeller automates all three by flagging injury cascades in real time, normalizing stats to possessions and pace, and running no-vig analysis across multiple books to surface mispriced lines before they correct.
