Pick6 Strategy: How to Build Winning Entries

DraftKings Pick6 is a player prop pick'em contest where you select between 2 and 6 props, calling each one higher or lower than the posted line. Unlike traditional parlays, Pick6 offers fixed multipliers and a structured format that rewards both accuracy and entry construction. The key to consistent success is not picking six random props — it is building entries around the highest-confidence signals available on that day's slate.

Start with Confidence, Not Names

The most common mistake Pick6 players make is anchoring entries around popular player names rather than statistical edge. A well-known star with a 52% confidence score is a weaker leg than a role player with a 67% confidence score. PropEdge sorts every analyzed prop by confidence, so the picks at the top of this page represent the strongest AI signals across all agents for today's slate — regardless of how recognizable the player's name is.

Use 3–4 Anchor Legs, Then Add Supporting Picks

The most effective Pick6 strategy for players new to the format is to identify the top 3 to 4 confidence picks as the core of the entry, then evaluate whether a 5th or 6th leg is genuinely strong enough to include. Adding a low-confidence leg to chase a higher multiplier often hurts expected value. PropEdge confidence scores make this decision concrete: legs above 60% are considered strong anchors, while legs between 55% and 60% are borderline additions worth including only when the rest of the entry is already solid.

Avoid Correlated Game Stacks in the Same Entry

Stacking multiple props from the same game introduces correlated risk. If the game goes to a blowout, all players on the losing team may see shortened minutes and reduced stat totals. PropEdge's parlay builder inside the app flags when two picks come from the same game and provides a risk grade for each combination, helping you build cross-sport Pick6 entries that reduce exposure to any single game outcome.
