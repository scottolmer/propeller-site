How PrizePicks Payouts Work

Power Play is all-or-nothing. Every pick must hit to win. Higher multipliers, higher risk. A 4-pick Power Play pays 10x — but miss one and you get nothing.

Flex Play lets you win even if some picks miss. You get reduced multipliers but profit is possible with partial accuracy. A 5-pick Flex pays 10x for a perfect 5/5, but still returns 2x for 4/5 and 0.4x (partial refund) for 3/5.

The tradeoff is clear: Power Play for max upside when you're confident, Flex Play for consistency when you want to reduce variance.

Win Probability & Expected Value

Your expected payout depends on your per-pick win rate. At 50% (coin flip), a 4-pick Power Play has a 6.25% chance of hitting. At 65% (Propeller's verified rate), that jumps to 17.9% — nearly 3x more likely.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does PrizePicks pay for a 4-pick Power Play?

A 4-pick Power Play on PrizePicks pays 10x your entry. A $10 entry returns $100 if all 4 picks hit.

What is the PrizePicks Flex Play payout?

Flex Play lets you win even if not all picks hit. A 5-pick Flex Play pays 10x for 5/5, 2x for 4/5, and 0.4x for 3/5.

What is the maximum PrizePicks payout?

The maximum multiplier on PrizePicks is 25x on a 6-pick Power Play. With a $100 entry, that's a $2,500 payout.

Is Flex Play or Power Play better on PrizePicks?

Flex Play has lower max payouts but lets you profit even missing 1-2 picks. Power Play pays more but requires all picks to hit. Flex Play is better for consistency, Power Play for max upside.
