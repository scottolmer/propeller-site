How PrizePicks Payouts Work

Power Play is all-or-nothing. Every pick must hit to win. Higher multipliers, higher risk. A 4-pick Power Play pays 10x — but miss one and you get nothing.

Flex Play lets you win even if some picks miss. You get reduced multipliers but profit is possible with partial accuracy. A 5-pick Flex pays 10x for a perfect 5/5, but still returns 2x for 4/5 and 0.4x (partial refund) for 3/5.

The tradeoff is clear: Power Play for max upside when you're confident, Flex Play for consistency when you want to reduce variance.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does PrizePicks pay for a 4-pick Power Play?

A 4-pick Power Play on PrizePicks pays 10x your entry. A $10 entry returns $100 if all 4 picks hit.

How many picks can you have on PrizePicks Power Play in 2026?

PrizePicks Power Play entries can have 2 to 6 picks. A 6-pick Power Play is the maximum standard card; every pick must hit for the 37.5x payout shown here.

What happens if you get 5 out of 6 on PrizePicks?

On a 6-pick Power Play, 5 out of 6 does not pay because every pick must hit. On a 6-pick Flex Play, the standard chart shown here pays 2x for 5/6.

What is the PrizePicks Flex Play payout?

Flex Play lets you win even if not all picks hit. A 6-pick Flex Play pays 25x for 6/6, 2x for 5/6, and 0.4x for 4/6. A 5-pick Flex Play pays 10x for 5/5, 2x for 4/5, and 0.4x for 3/5.

What is the maximum PrizePicks payout?

The maximum standard multiplier on PrizePicks is 37.5x on a 6-pick Power Play. With a $100 entry, that is a $3,750 payout before any app-specific restrictions, promotions, or payout changes.

When does PrizePicks payout?

PrizePicks payouts generally post to your account balance after the lineup is graded, often within a few minutes. Bonus Lineup payouts and promotional entries can have different balance treatment, so check the final grading and balance details in the PrizePicks app.

Is Flex Play or Power Play better on PrizePicks?

Flex Play has lower max payouts but lets you profit even missing 1-2 picks. Power Play pays more but requires all picks to hit. Flex Play is better for consistency, Power Play for max upside.
