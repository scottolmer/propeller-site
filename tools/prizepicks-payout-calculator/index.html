How this PrizePicks payout calculator works

This calculator applies PrizePicks' published standard Player Pick payout rates to your entry amount. It can calculate standard Power Play and Flex Play returns and illustrate scenario probabilities using a hit rate you supply.

What it cannot calculate

It cannot determine a lineup's final payout when special projections, promotions, pick combinations, fees, Team Picks, Culture Picks, or other disclosed factors change the multiplier.

DNPs and Reboots

A DNP or eligible Reboot removes the affected projection and normally reverts the lineup down one pick tier. A 2-pick Power reverts to a refund.

Tied projections

A tie lowers the potential payout by one tier, but unlike a DNP or Reboot, it does not remove the projection or trigger same-team or minimum-pick ineligibility.

The final screen controls

PrizePicks discloses the payout for each contest on its individual details screen. Review that displayed payout before submitting because it controls over this general calculator.

How PrizePicks Payouts Work

Power Play requires every active pick to be correct for the listed standard payout. At the standard Player Pick rates, a 4-pick Power Play returns 10x when all four picks are correct.

Flex Play has more than one payout tier. At the standard rates, a 5-pick Flex returns 10x for 5 correct, 2x for 4 correct, and 0.4x for 3 correct.

These formats use different payout structures. This calculator reports the published standard rates without recommending one entry type over another.

A projection that finishes exactly on its listed number lowers the potential payout by one tier.
