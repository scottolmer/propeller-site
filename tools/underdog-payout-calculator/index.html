Standard vs Flex: When to Use Each

Standard is all-or-nothing with higher multipliers. Use it when you have high-confidence picks and want maximum payout. Best for 2–4 pick entries where your hit rate supports the math.

Flex provides a safety net — you can still profit missing 1–2 picks. The tradeoff is lower max multipliers (e.g., 10x vs 20x on 5-picks). Use Flex for 5+ pick entries where the probability of a perfect sweep drops significantly.

Use the calculator's hit-rate field to test your own assumptions. Standard rewards perfect cards with higher multipliers, while Flex gives you a safety net when the chance of a perfect sweep drops.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Underdog pay for a 4-pick Standard entry?

A 4-pick Standard entry on Underdog pays 10x your entry. A $10 entry returns $100 if all 4 picks hit.

How does Underdog payout work?

Underdog payouts depend on entry size, Standard vs Flex mode, and the final payout shown before you submit. Standard entries require every pick to hit. Flex entries can pay reduced amounts when one or more picks miss.

How much do Underdog picks pay out?

Underdog payouts depend on entry size and whether you choose Standard or Flex. The 2026 chart shown here ranges from 3.5x for a 2-pick Standard entry to 120x for an 8-pick Standard entry.

What is the Underdog Flex payout?

Underdog Flex lets you win even if some picks miss. Displayed Flex amounts can be up-to payouts, and modifiers or entry terms may change the final amount, so confirm the payout on the final entry screen.

When does Underdog Fantasy payout?

Underdog contest winnings generally become available after the entry is graded. Withdrawable balance can depend on bonus, promotion, program credit, or playthrough terms, so check your account balance details after grading.

What is the maximum Underdog payout?

The maximum multiplier on Underdog is 120x on an 8-pick Standard entry. With a $50 entry, that's a $6,000 payout.

Is Underdog Standard or Flex better?

Standard pays more but requires every pick to hit. Flex provides a safety net — you can still profit missing 1-2 picks. Flex is better for bankroll management, Standard for max upside.
