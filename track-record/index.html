The grading path

From stored analysis to historical result.

The current archive documents its grading path while being explicit about what the legacy data cannot prove.

01

Store the analysis row

The public feed exposes player, market, line, direction, signal score, sport, game date, and book. It does not expose a reliable publication timestamp for every historical row.

02

Grade against the final stat

Overs need the final stat to finish above the line. Unders need it below the line. Exact ties are pushes.

03

Refresh the historical archive

Wins, losses, pushes, sport splits, and signal ranges refresh from the public APIs. Raw summaries and collapsed ledger entries remain separate units.

What the record means.

The goal is not to make the number sound bigger. The goal is to make the number understandable.

Pushes excluded

Pushes are shown in the record, but they are tracked separately from wins and losses.

Sample size matters

A large row count can add research context, but repeated observations do not equal the same number of independent recommendations.

Confidence is not certainty

A confidence score is a directional research signal, not a calibrated probability or guarantee unless a page explicitly says otherwise.

Prospective record is separate

New public recommendations will use an immutable pre-event publication contract rather than being backfilled from the mixed historical archive. Read the grading answer and corrections policy.
