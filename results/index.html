How grading works

The grading rule is simple; the archive needs context.

Rows are graded after games finish by comparing the stored side and line with the final stat. Pushes remain separate. Publication time, model version, evaluation mode, and consistent price data are not available on every historical row.

01

Analysis row stored

The historical feed exposes player, market, line, direction, signal score, sport, game date, and book. It does not establish pre-event publication for every row.

02

Outcome graded

Final stat values are compared to the line. Overs win above the line, unders win below the line, and exact ties are pushes.

03

Record updated

Wins, losses, pushes, totals, and sport splits refresh from the public results API.

Limitations and definitions

What you should know before quoting this archive.

The historical database and collapsed ledger are research records. Neither is a clean cohort of uniquely published recommendations. See how grading works and the editorial and corrections policy.

01

Two different units

The summary and confidence endpoints count raw graded analysis rows. The public ledger applies a collapse key and therefore reports a smaller total.

02

Historical data is mixed

Repeated snapshots and legacy retrospective analysis are included. The current feed does not label every row as forward-tested or backtested.

03

No ROI claim

Price and payout are not consistently present, so the descriptive outcome rate cannot support a defensible profit or return-on-investment claim.
