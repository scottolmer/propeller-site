The Basics

What is Pick6?

Pick6 is DraftKings' pick'em game where you select between 2 and 6 player props — each a simple over or under on a stat line — and combine them into a single entry. The more picks you include, the higher the payout multiplier. A 2-pick entry pays 3x your stake; a 6-pick entry can return 25x or more.

Unlike traditional daily fantasy sports, there is no salary cap or roster construction. You are purely picking whether individual players go over or under their number on a given stat. That simplicity is Pick6's appeal — but it also means every single pick must carry genuine edge, because one miss in a 6-pick entry erases the whole ticket.

The lines are set by DraftKings to be difficult to beat consistently. The players who do beat them over time are not luckier — they are more rigorous. They check injuries before locking in, they understand matchup context, and they only play picks where the data supports a clear directional edge. That is exactly what this guide — and PropEdge — helps you do.

Pick6 Tips

7 Pick6 Strategies That Actually Work

Tip 01

Use Data, Not Gut Feelings

The most consistent Pick6 winners treat every entry like an investment decision. That means looking at a player's recent stat trend, the defensive quality of their opponent, the game context (pace, game total, home/away), and the injury situation — before touching a pick. Gut feel is not noise-free. Data is. When you have 8 independent analytical signals all pointing the same direction, that is a real edge. When only two agree, pass.

Tip 02

Focus on High-Confidence Props Only

Not all props are created equal. Some have clear, well-supported edges — a receiver matched up against the league's worst secondary, playing indoors in a high-total game, coming off three straight games over his number. Others are coin flips dressed up as opportunities. Identify which props have multi-factor support and only include those in your entries. Picking a 6-leg entry means needing six genuinely strong picks, not six guesses.

Tip 03

Always Check Injuries Before Locking In

A single injury report can flip a prop completely. A point guard going from doubtful to out does not just affect his own points line — it changes the usage distribution for every teammate. A running back's target share drops when the receiving back returns from injury. These are second-order effects that most players miss. Check injury reports within two hours of game time, every time, for every player in your entry.

Tip 04

Never Ignore Matchup Context

A running back against a team giving up the second-most rushing yards per game is a fundamentally different pick than that same running back against a top-5 run defense. Line setters know this too, but the market often under- or over-adjusts. Understanding which defenses are genuinely bad versus which ones just had a bad week is the kind of edge that compounds over hundreds of picks.

Tip 05

Mix Entry Sizes Strategically

A 2-pick entry pays 3x and needs both picks to hit. A 6-pick entry pays 25x and needs all six. The math suggests you should build your 6-pick entries from your absolute highest-confidence picks and keep them small, while using moderate stakes on 2- and 3-pick entries from your strongest daily signals. Never fill out a 6-leg entry with mediocre picks just to chase the payout — that is how bankrolls disappear.

Tip 06

Compare Lines Across Platforms

DraftKings sets its own lines for Pick6. Those lines are not always identical to what FanDuel, PrizePicks, or Underdog are offering on the same prop. A player with a 25.5 points line on Pick6 might be at 24.5 on a competitor. That half-point difference can be the difference between a winning entry and a push. Line shopping takes 60 seconds and is one of the highest-value habits a serious player can build.

Tip 07

Track Your Results and Learn from Losses

Most pick'em players have no idea what their actual win rate is. Without tracking, you cannot tell if you are improving, what bet types work best for you, or which sports you have a genuine edge in. Log every pick, every result. After 50 entries you will start seeing patterns. After 100 you will know exactly where your edge is — and where you are giving money away.
