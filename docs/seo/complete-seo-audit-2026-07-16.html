Technical and indexation findings

Confirmed issues, ordered by impact

Legacy sport aliases are full indexable duplicates, not redirects

High
Evidence
/mlb/, /nba/, /nfl/, /nhl/, and /soccer/ each return HTTP 200 with index,follow, full duplicate content, and a canonical to /picks/{sport}/. They are absent from the sitemap and internal link graph, but remain crawlable.
Impact
Canonical tags are hints, not redirects. Google can still crawl the duplicates, split signals temporarily, or select an unexpected canonical. The current production state also contradicts the internal July strategy's statement that these are noindex redirects.
Fix
Implement permanent HTTP 301/308 redirects at the hosting edge. If GitHub Pages prevents that, ship minimal alias documents with noindex,follow, canonical, immediate accessible link, and a tested client redirect as an interim measure.

The XML sitemap includes seven non-HTML discovery assets

Medium
Evidence
The 62-URL sitemap contains 55 HTML pages plus llms.txt, pricing.md, and five JSON endpoints.
Impact
A Google XML sitemap should primarily enumerate canonical URLs intended for search results. AI manifests and machine-readable datasets can remain public and linked without asking Google to index them as landing pages.
Fix
Remove the seven machine-readable/text assets from the Google sitemap unless there is a deliberate search-indexing goal. Preserve them in llms.txt, data/index.json, and visible documentation links.

Prominent internal links point to noindex player pages

Medium
Evidence
2,699 local player/archive pages are noindex. Twenty-four links from indexable sport/analyzer pages point to noindex player profiles, while scripts/analyzer_indexed.txt currently contains no qualifying URLs.
Impact
The conservative index policy is correct, but “Popular Player Analysis” links currently route crawl and user attention to pages the site has decided do not deserve search indexing. That weakens the purpose of the module.
Fix
Do not mass-index the cohort. Either select a small evidence-qualified set with current unique utility, or change the module to link to canonical sport/analyzer states until individual pages pass freshness, data depth, and demand gates.

One cited legal/industry source is dead

Medium
Evidence
https://www.legal-sports-report.com/dfs/ fails DNS resolution and is linked from the PrizePicks and Pick6 strategy guides.
Impact
The broken citation weakens a sensitive legal-classification claim and creates a poor trust signal.
Fix
Remove the broad legal conclusion or replace it with current first-party rules and state-eligibility sources. Add an automated outbound-link check for editorially important sources.

Security headers are minimal

Low
Evidence
The GitHub Pages response serves HTTPS and gzip, but the sampled homepage response did not include HSTS, CSP, X-Content-Type-Options, Referrer-Policy, or Permissions-Policy.
Impact
This is primarily a security hardening issue, not a direct ranking defect. HTTPS itself is working correctly.
Fix
If moving behind a configurable CDN/edge, add appropriate headers after testing analytics, app links, fonts, and third-party scripts. Do not prioritize this over claim corrections or performance.
On-page, content, and trust

The content problem is precision, not length

A stale win-rate claim appears in a search snippet surface

High
Evidence
The live /picks/mlb/ meta description says: 67% win rate on 14K+ graded picks. The current product contract distinguishes the historical archive from a prospective performance claim and does not support this undated headline statistic.
Impact
This can be shown directly in search results. It conflicts with the site's transparent-record positioning and creates advertising, trust, and reputational risk.
Fix
Replace it immediately with current, non-performance language. Add a regression check that rejects fixed win-rate, accuracy, ROI, and profitability claims in titles, descriptions, Open Graph, schema, and visible copy unless sourced from a dated approved ledger field.

Several guides overstate certainty and profitability

High
Evidence
The player-prop guide says systematic analysis separates “profitable prop analysis from guessing,” cross-book disagreement is “exploitable,” injury news is the “highest-edge window,” and selective high-confidence volume “consistently outperforms.” The NBA guide similarly claims “exploitable inefficiency” and improved long-term results.
Impact
These are outcome claims without cited Propeller evidence. In a gambling-adjacent topic, overconfidence directly harms trustworthiness and conflicts with the site's own “research, not sportsbook” positioning.
Fix
Rewrite as conditional research guidance: disagreement indicates uncertainty, not guaranteed value; confidence is not calibrated probability; any performance proposition needs a defined sample, price, period, baseline, and public evidence.

The no-vig explanation contains a mathematical error

High
Evidence
The NBA guide states that a -110/-110 market reveals approximately 52.4% per side after removing vig. In fact, 52.38% is each side's raw implied probability; normalizing both sides removes the overround and produces 50% per side.
Impact
This is a concrete factual error in the site's core expertise area and is duplicated in visible content and schema answers.
Fix
Correct every visible and JSON-LD instance from the source/generator, cite the formula, and add unit-tested examples for two-way and asymmetric markets. Avoid calling a no-vig estimate “true probability.”

Authorship is organizational where first-hand expertise would help

Medium
Evidence
Guides typically use “Written by the Propeller team.” The editorial policy and methodology are strong, but named reviewer expertise is not consistently visible on high-stakes strategy and statistical explanations.
Impact
Organizational authorship is not inherently wrong, but named review, sources, and correction history would make expertise and accountability easier to evaluate.
Fix
Add a real named author or subject-matter reviewer only where truthful. Show review date, source list, correction policy, and methodology version. Do not invent credentials.

Structured data

Generic analyzer category ownership remains unproven

High
Evidence
A fresh generic “free AI player prop analyzer” spot check surfaced competing tools, while Propeller appeared for branded and calculator/guide queries. The July AEO snapshot recorded zero mentions for “best AI player prop analyzer” in completed runs and weak visibility for generic free-analyzer questions.
Impact
The product page is not yet the obvious search/answer-engine owner of the product's primary category. Platform and utility pages are doing more acquisition work.
Fix
Do not create another generic analyzer URL. Improve /analyzer/ performance and demonstrated utility, link to it with descriptive anchors from relevant high-authority utility pages, monitor query-to-page movement, and earn independent citations around the benchmark/methodology.

Low CTR is an opportunity, not permission for constant rewrites

Medium
Evidence
GSC reports 50,622 impressions, 378 clicks, 0.75% CTR, and average position 7.7. Underdog was 0.68% CTR at position 11.3; MLB was 0.37% at position 8.2. Several titles/descriptions were changed only recently.
Impact
There is upside in query-to-snippet alignment, but repeated edits before enough post-release data accumulates will destroy learnings.
Fix
Freeze recently changed snippets for a defined observation window. Test only high-impression page/query cohorts, log the change date, and judge clicks plus qualified analyzer/signup behavior—not CTR alone.

Authority is the limiting moat

Medium
Evidence
The current AEO matrix shows strong Perplexity visibility but no completed Copilot runs, only 15 ChatGPT runs, and incomplete coverage overall (195/300). Competitors and publishers are repeatedly cited for generic analyzer and platform-research intent.
Impact
Owned-page optimization alone cannot manufacture third-party authority. The incomplete matrix also prevents a complete cross-engine conclusion.
Fix
Complete the frozen 300-observation benchmark, correct owned facts first, then build an editorially qualified publisher citation program around calculators, transparent methodology, benchmark data, and the prospective record. No manufactured community mentions.
Prioritized action plan

What to do next

Within 48 hours: remove factual and performance-risk claims

Delete the MLB “67% on 14K+” description, correct the -110 no-vig math, soften unsupported profitability/edge/outperformance language, and replace the dead legal citation. Regenerate visible copy and schema together.

Within 7 days: make URL ownership real

Deploy true permanent redirects for all five legacy sport aliases. Remove non-landing assets from the Google sitemap. Add regression checks for alias status/directives and forbidden unsourced performance claims.

Within 14 days: improve the analyzer template

Profile critical CSS, fonts, analytics, and unused JavaScript. Target the actual LCP path. Re-run mobile Lighthouse on homepage, analyzer, one calculator, one guide, and one picks page; then compare with field CWV.

Within 14 days: resolve the noindex-player link mismatch

Either qualify a small player cohort using current unique value and demand, or route prominent modules to canonical sport/analyzer experiences. Keep empty, stale, and repetitive player pages noindex.

Over 30 days: measure before expanding

Use page/query GSC cohorts for analyzer ownership, calculator CTR, MLB, Underdog, and comparisons. Complete the 300-row AEO run. Do not clone new sport modules or generic analyzer pages without measured demand and distinct utility.

Over 30–90 days: earn authority

Create a qualified publisher prospect list and evidence-to-pitch map. Lead with verifiable calculators, methodology, benchmark, and prospective-record assets. Track earned links/citations, assisted analyzer use, and qualified signups.

Limits and untested areas

What this audit cannot honestly claim

Index coverage

No fresh Search Console Page Indexing export or URL Inspection sample was available in the workspace. The crawl proves accessibility, not that Google indexed every intended URL or accepted every canonical.

Field Core Web Vitals

CrUX/PageSpeed field data could not be retrieved because the public API quota was exhausted. Lighthouse is directional lab evidence only.

Backlink profile

No Ahrefs, Semrush, Majestic, or Search Console Links export was provided. This report does not invent domain authority, link counts, toxic-link findings, or a disavow recommendation.

Rank tracking

Live result checks are volatile, localized spot checks. GSC is the source of truth for Propeller's measured queries and pages; a single observed result is not a stable ranking report.

International SEO

The audited site is US-English and does not expose locale variants. Hreflang is not required under the current single-language architecture.

Rich-result eligibility

Structured data is valid locally, but Google controls feature eligibility. FAQ rich results are generally not expected for this site type; no traffic lift is promised.
